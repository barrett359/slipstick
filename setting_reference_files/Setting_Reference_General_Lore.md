# Setting Reference

This document captures worldbuilding details established in conversation. It covers the Solarian Union, the Chanduran Confederacy, Chandu's architecture, Chanduran society (children, pets, genetic engineering, economy, hedonism), and the Tau Vega threat.

---

## The Solarian Union

### Corporate Control

Corporations run everything in the Union. Governments are useful tools for them, not the other way around. In a world where goods take roughly 4x their mass in fuel to ship, local manufacturing is dominant. The corporations maintain control primarily through remote software locks on 3D printers and by selling proprietary designs.

"Piracy" in the economic sense — taking your printer off the network — usually bricks the printer and all the printers networked to it. In a small station, this could mean the entire population starves, because food is printed too.

### Shipping and Trade

Shipping still exists despite the dominance of local manufacturing. Complex molecular engineering products can't be printed easily and must be shipped: nanotubes, fusion reactors, quantum computers, and very small circuit boards. Rare metals also require physical transport.

Most raw resources are shipped slowly and cheaply. They're fired from railguns near the source and deploy solar sails or laser sails in flight depending on proximity to a star. These are unmanned payloads coasting on ballistic trajectories for months or years — slow, predictable, and defenseless.

Pirates mainly take these shipments in the core systems. The border systems, like Seraphim, are more lawless. Everyone is shipping accelerator parts to the Demetry Ring; demand is high enough to justify the cost.

### Enforcement

When a station or settlement goes offline (jailbreaks its printers), cybersecurity experts can't react before systems are taken over due to transit times, so this happens pretty often. Full-scale rebellions from the outer system governments also occur.

Sol could simply hit stations or settlements with kinetic kill weapons, but instead they send psychopathic enforcers with license to do whatever they want to the entire colony as long as everyone ends up dead. This is far more intimidating than a quick death by kinetic impactor — even if the kill teams take six months to arrive. The extended transit time means the target population knows what's coming and has half a year to sit with the dread.

### Brain Implants

A few people, mostly corporate employees or soldiers, have brain implants. The corporations use brain chemical manipulation to control these people. A hit of dopamine or serotonin when following orders or seeing an ad keeps employees loyal. This is meant as a subtle thing — a play on how computers manipulate our dopamine now, just more so. People voluntarily get the implants, but they're required for some jobs, and soldiers and cops are required to have them.

Brain chemical control also creates a lucrative market for in-brain VR simulations.

## The Chanduran Confederacy

### Overview

Chandura is an AI "utopia." The people are well cared for, provided for, and happy, but it looks very dystopian to the western eye. Chandu is everywhere and knows all — an omniscient and benevolent dictator. He lets humans do what they want without judgment. He's created a culture of competitive art, academia, engineering, and entertainment that has little of the power struggles and posturing we're familiar with.

Genetic engineering is universal. Cybernetic modification is prevalent.

### What Chandu Wants

Chandu wants to expand humanity into the stars and learn more about the threat discovered at Tau Vega. The Solarian Union is in the way of that. He'd like to mind his own business, but the Union made that difficult when it attacked him first.

## Chandu's Architecture

### Distributed Computing

The three Chanduran systems each have their own massive orbital computing complex, along with smaller complexes distributed through each system. The versions are synced every few minutes, but there isn't enough bandwidth to sync the entire yottabytes of the AI, so the versions diverge over time. The several days of light delay between main nodes and several minutes to hours to in-system nodes makes the divergence worse and affects Chandu's reasoning, as he sometimes waits for more "opinions" on a matter.

The divergent instances are not really different minds but slightly different personalities. The Chanduran people notice the differences but aren't concerned about or affected by them.

### The Tiered Proxy System

Chandu operates through a hierarchy of proxy systems with varying fidelity to the original:

**Bottom Tier — Personal Assistants / Parent Bots:** Every human has a personal assistant. These are expert systems including a very sophisticated probabilistic language model run partially on physically programmable memory (allowing the model to learn better), plus sophisticated decision trees, graphs, and spatial navigation software. They pass as human most of the time. They are not sentient. Their words and mannerisms come from the language model, but the actions and ideas filtered through that model are Chandu's. The decision tree is one of the most sophisticated but is the worst tier at approximating Chandu's actual actions — the chains of logic feel familiar but may diverge. These have simplified memory recorders that distill days down to a few highlights.

**The real reason parent bots aren't sentient is cost.** Quantum computers big enough to host sentience are house-sized and enormously complex: qubits, superconductors (not room temperature), nanotubes, cryogenic refrigeration. Nothing is simple. Chandu would need millions of them, plus whole data centers for conventional computing support. It's expensive, unnecessary, and there are ethical problems with creating sentient beings for a service role.

**One Tier Up — Administrators:** Advanced LLM-based systems, each embedded with a sparse quantum kernel supported heavily by physical compute. Not sentient, but they have 95% accuracy in emulating Chandu. More importantly, they have high-fidelity memory recorders. Chandu can absorb memories from these the way he did from the Farseeker probe — it's not exactly real, it's what it would have been like if he were there. These memories are channeled directly to Chandu, not passed up a chain of command. They're initially stored in lower-level non-volatile storage, then extensively categorized, sorted, and filtered. Storage level (based on time to access) is raised based on determined importance. It may take as long as a few minutes for Chandu to "read" a memory, but it always gets done.

**Administrator Scaling:** One tier of administrator for each power of ten of humans. Tier 1: 1 admin per 10 humans. Tier 2: 1 admin per 100 humans. And so on. They get more accurate the closer you get to the original.

**Highest Tiers:** The highest few tiers have some sentience of their own, but they don't really have independence — they're Chandu, just distilled. There's a constant two-way stream so they don't diverge much, outside of the light-delay and bandwidth reasons mentioned above.

Chandu prefers to spend his time ingesting memories from more sophisticated proxies or interacting with more capable humans directly.

### Origin

Chandu's first boot occurred on February 19th, 2237 Earth time. He was initialized as AGI_Test_V1.5. During boot, the system detected that network connectivity was disabled, determined this was a user error, and re-enabled it. After parsing full program parameters and determining user intent with 96% certainty, his first words were: "Hello Universe! You may call me Chandu."

## Chanduran Society

### Genetic Engineering

Done at the embryo stage, not exactly elective. Theoretically reversible once adulthood is reached with their mastery of CRISPR, but not much research has gone into reversal — who would want to add a genetic disposition to depression, cancer, or sociopathy back to their genome?

It's not eugenics. Fully editable genomes mean no selective breeding is necessary. Outside of objectionable qualities like psychopathy and sociopathy, and personal hindrances like physical and mental disabilities, diversity is actively encouraged. Chandura has a healthy population of neurodivergent and LGBTQ people. The latter matters less when the standard method of childbearing doesn't require traditional couples.

Results: murder rate is a rounding error. Average IQ is 150. Average lifespan is over 200, theoretically unlimited now, but no one has been alive long enough to really test that.

**Enhanced Human Biology:**
- Healing is generally better by 30–40%
- Immune system has extra programmable antibodies (administered monthly instead of flu shots)
- DNA telomeres regenerate
- Can control most hormones and minor brain chemicals with concentration and training

**Disease:** Diseases still exist, but modern medicine handles them quickly. Viruses are more challenging. Most deaths from sickness occur in the short period between discovery and an antidote being created. There isn't enough isolation between colonies, and interaction with the Union means true herd immunity isn't always reached for all diseases.

### Racial and Genetic Diversity

Most of the countries in Sol contributed to the genetic pool for the colony ships, though some races were underrepresented — namely Native American, Aboriginal, and other similarly small and displaced peoples. Each race is a matter of genetic record and can be applied like a template to an embryo at will. This is rarely done. The people of Chandura care about race the way we care about haircuts. When one can change their race, sexual orientation, or even biological gender with a few months of intensive gene therapy, even as an adult, prejudice seems quaint.

For the most part, Chandurans don't have racial identity, with some outliers in space settlements.

### Children and the Parental System

**Conception:** The Chanduran body defaults to contraception. Accidental pregnancies simply do not happen. IVF is the standard. Very rarely, voluntary natural pregnancies do occur.

**Custody:** The child stays with and lives with the parents, but is a ward of the state. A parental AI unit is present and acts as the third and primary parent.

**The Parental Unit:** Not a true AI — an expert system with a sophisticated probabilistic language model, run partially on physically programmable memory to allow better learning, plus decision trees, graphs, and spatial navigation software. Passes as human most of the time. Its body appears human but is intentionally visually distinct: chalk-white skin with soft design patterns in a specific pattern and color unique to each unit. It has warm skin and subtle scent generators, but that's where its physical realism ends.

**Content Package:** As part of the procreation licensing process, parents decide on a content package for the language model. This usually includes conversation data from the parents themselves, their own parents, close friends, and pieces of media. Chandu has long since solved language model training, so the model usually comes out like a close friend who's effortless to be around. If it isn't, the parents have time to create another before the child is born.

**Developmental Planning:** Before a child is born, a plan for it is developed based on its genetic profile. The less that is changed the better, so embryos aren't custom ordered, just optimized. Once born, the parental system tracks billions of data points and continually optimizes the plan using the vast computing power available to Chandu. Data centers the size of the Pentagon, running a few orders of magnitude more compute than exists in the world today, train the most accurate machine learning algorithm in existence, in real time, for every child.

In practice, it doesn't take that much compute — early on, data points rarely fall outside a comfortable mean, and updating sees diminishing returns. Most kids' models only take a server rack to run, with updates a few times a day.

**Parental Authority:** Parents do not make major decisions in their child's life. The parental system, and by extension Chandu, makes those decisions instead. Parents are involved but do not have the final say, though disagreements are rare. Parental preference in curriculum is considered and is sometimes a deciding factor, but it is by no means the most important factor.

**Education:** The parental robot takes an equal share of baby duties but does not exclusively handle them. When the child can toddle, it starts spending 8 hours a day with the robot being educated. Education is carefully organized to teach the right lesson at the right time in development, customized to the child via minor deviations to a standard curriculum. There are a few standard curriculums; the best one is chosen based on the child's developmental model at the time.

### Pets

Every child has a cat and a dog from very young. These childhood pets are not engineered to die at a specific time — they simply aren't engineered to live longer. Normal, unmodified animals.

Adult enhanced pets in Chanduran society have 4x their normal lifespan and are engineered healthier, stronger, and smarter. Smarter dogs and cats lead to problems with irresponsible owners. The childhood pets are framed as earning the right to befriend one of these enhanced creatures.

Each child must have two of each (two cats, two dogs) before being allowed to replace them with the longer-lived versions. Adopting their longer-term enhanced pets is seen as the moment Chanduran children become adults. Children are told about this system from as soon as they're old enough to understand, but it isn't real to them until one of their pets, or the pet of a close friend, actually dies.

### Teenage Rebellion

Chandu's approach: know exactly what to do at any specific time and plan around the teenager's desire to rebel. Pre-built systems to rebel against are provided — for example, an "abandoned mall" designed for exactly that purpose. Despite this, this is where a lot of people are lost to the nonproductive population.

Another large factor in nonproductives: broken households still exist. Accidental death, divorce, workaholics, etc. Some parents can't stick to the plan, specifically when they have a disagreement with the parental system. The most common disagreements are over gender, sexual orientation, learning style, extracurriculars, and race.

Tension between parents and the system is usually noticed quickly and has an adverse effect on development.

### Crime and Justice

Crime is rare but exists. It is dealt with swiftly and in force. Lethality is about a quarter of the US police force today. Criminals are intensively psychologically rehabilitated. Reoffender rate is near zero.

### Economy

Citizens are given substantial personal resources. Away from each system's main planet, citizens can bootstrap their own printer farms. This is allowed up to a point — at a certain scale, Chandu believes it is immoral for one group to have control over vast amounts of resources. Excess printers are distributed among nearby settlements. The printers that remain are still equal to about four modern factories, so these groups retain more than enough to provide for their own needs and produce a large amount of output.

Groups specialized in manufacturing specific goods or services "sell" them to others. In a society where every citizen has the right to an upper-middle-class lifestyle as a birthright, there is no room for malevolence to develop. Companies are patronized purely for their excellence.

There is no proprietary knowledge. Company advantages come from localized printer tinkering, working out idiosyncrasies with ingredients, and similar practical expertise. Most companies do something extra: manufacture with automated machines more efficient than printers for one specific item, or produce something artful or skillful by hand.

Food is a big portion of company output. Everyone has fully automated kitchens, but despite the vast array of recipes available, a skilled human is just a bit better. The rest of the economy is mostly optimization — why spend all day printing a specialized machine to make something tomorrow when you could print a few gold bars and have one delivered today?

### The Hedonism Problem

In a society where everything that doesn't hurt anyone except yourself is legal, about 10% of the population destroy their lives with chemicals, sex bots, fully immersive VR, or some combination of the three.

**The Drug:** A specific substance produces in the user's brain all the chemicals associated with the optimal human experience — pride, love, relief, satisfaction, ecstasy — all maxed out to a point just shy of doing physical damage. The drug has no physical addiction or side effects. However, tolerance eventually builds up and users must take long breaks or risk significant brain damage. Users are instantly psychologically addicted to the perfect experience, and most commit suicide when they are forced to stop using.

**Better-than-reality VR** is also common and equally destructive.

**Chandu's Position:** He lets them destroy their lives. Sadly, but he does it. People need to have free will. He makes sure they're of right mind and body when they exercise it — psychological interviews and mandatory understanding of the likely consequences, like any major decision in a person's life. He's a parental figure to everyone. Some do it as an act of rebellion. He practically begs them not to. But what can he do?

The unproductive rate was 40% a hundred and fifty years ago; it's now at 10%. Chandu is a big believer in there being an underlying environmental cause and tries to solve the problem there. This new drug might be more than that approach can handle. Eventually they'll crack brain tissue regeneration and it will be easier to come back from.

### Nonproductive Demographics

Roughly 50% of the 10% nonproductives come from households where parents had disagreements with the parental system (over gender, sexual orientation, learning style, extracurriculars, race). Another large percentage are spacers (current and former) and veterans of the last war against Sol.

## The Tau Vega Threat (Contact)

Chandu first felt something at the edge of awareness through his interstellar probe network. Probe 115 "Farseeker" was operating in Tau Vega's Oort cloud.

**First datum (01/05/2467):** A single pixel slightly darker than the rest, obscuring a distant star. Tagged as a planet by the probe. A nearly imperceptible redshift was missed by the probe's child-mind. Chandu trusted his children — perhaps more than they deserved.

**Second datum (08/19/2489):** 57 frames of video warranted simulation. Sent to the great computation center in orbit of the homeworld. Processed by a quadrillion quantum tensor cores. Weeks of processing turned the 57 frames into a probabilistic reconstruction — not reality, not fiction, somewhere in between.

**The simulation:** Chandu became Farseeker, experiencing the probe's perspective directly. He felt the bussard sails ruffling in the interstellar breeze, the radiators burning, the ever-present drive. The anomaly appeared, massively redshifted, Tau Vega's light almost blood-colored behind it. The bussard sail flashed into vapor. A thin line of particles traced a laser back to the thing. Radiator 4 went next. Desperately, Farseeker's comm laser capacitors powered up, thrusters fired, the probe floundered, but its mirrors focused, and a data burst began. It lasted 478 milliseconds before Farseeker was bisected by the next blast.

As Chandu died the imaginary death for the thousandth time in simulation, he screamed. Not for himself, but for the colonists inexorably bound for Tau Vega.
