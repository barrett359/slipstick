**Metallic Hydrogen (Standard)**

ve = 16,700 m/s. Workhorse round, mass-produced. Self-heating recombination — no reactor, no external power. Simple combustion chamber and nozzle.

MR 10: ΔV = 38.5 km/s MR 20: ΔV = 50 km/s MR 40: ΔV = 61.6 km/s (diminishing returns, structural engineering becomes brutal at single-stage)

**Staged MH**

Staging drops dead engine and tank mass between phases, effectively allowing high mass ratios without the structural nightmare of a single MR 40 tank. Two stages at MR ~6.3 each achieve equivalent MR 40 in a buildable package. Numbers weren't fully worked out — flagged as a gap.

**Antimatter Thermal (Premium)**

Magnetic bottle nozzle gives tunable ISP by varying AM-to-propellant mix ratio. No physical nozzle thermal limit. No reactor mass.

MR 20 at ISP 3,000: ΔV = 75 km/s vs MH's 50 km/s. Real advantage is thrust-to-weight — lighter engine means higher acceleration throughout the burn. Mission profile drives ISP selection. High-accel interceptor uses low ISP, high propellant fraction. Long-range strategic round uses high ISP, small propellant fraction.

Pre-Ring: $10M/g makes AM missiles ~$80M each in fuel. Precision strike tier — fired at things that justify the expense. Post-Ring: $50k-$100k/g. Mass production becomes viable. This is what Chandura cannot allow.

**Fusion Bus (Platform-Launched Only)**

D-He3 drive, ISP ~20,000s. Too heavy for shipborne magazines — reactor, magnets, shielding add 500kg-1 tonne minimum to dry mass. Launches from stations, asteroid platforms, or dedicated tenders.

Profile: bus boosts for hours at 1-3g, accumulating 40-60 km/s. Carries 4-8 MH terminal missiles clamped to frame. Bus releases missiles at terminal range with full propellant reserves intact. Closing velocity through PD gauntlet: 90-110 km/s versus 25-50 km/s for ship-launched MH. Time under BB main laser fire at 25,000 km: ~230-280 seconds versus ~830 seconds. Roughly 3× less attrition.

Bus either expends or recovers depending on doctrine. Solarians expend it. Chandurans probably recover.

**Standard Burn Profile (All Shipborne Types)**

Three phases:

Boost (30-40% propellant): moderate acceleration, clears the launching ship, initial vector established. ΔV ~12-15 km/s.

Coast: dark, minimal thermal signature, ECM active. Covers most of the distance to target. Duration scales with launch range. Majority of transit time spent here.

Terminal (60-70% propellant remaining): high and rapidly rising acceleration as mass drops, aggressive jinking, ECM active, cluster bus separates, torplets disperse to saturate PD. ΔV ~35-38 km/s. This is the killing phase. PD kills it here or dies.

Reserving most propellant for terminal phase is the key design decision — the missile arrives with maneuvering reserves, not speed. Agility at the point of interception is what defeats PD, not raw velocity.

**Warhead Types**

Casaba Howitzer (Solarian standard): nuclear shaped charge, focused plasma jet. Standoff range 50-300 km depending on yield. Simple, reliable, no pointing requirement after detonation.

Bomb-pumped X-ray laser (Chanduran specialty): nuclear device pumps X-ray lasing rods. Fires at lightspeed, completes in ~10 nanoseconds before mechanical disruption from detonation. Potentially longer standoff than Casaba. Requires precise target knowledge at moment of detonation — no correction after firing. Beam divergence set by rod geometry, typically microradians.

**Inventory Tiers**

MH saturation rounds: cheap, mass-produced, most of the magazine. The wall of fire that overwhelms PD by numbers.

AM precision rounds: $50-80M each pre-Ring, potentially mass-producible post-Ring. Fired at high-value targets, not corvettes.

Fusion-boosted strategic packages: platform assets, not ship assets. Campaign-level weapons. The thing hidden behind Gabriel's horizon.

Interceptors: smaller and cheaper than offensive rounds. No Casaba warhead needed — proximity frag and enough delta-V to reach the intercept point. 100-300 kg each. Ships carry many more interceptors than offensive missiles by count.
